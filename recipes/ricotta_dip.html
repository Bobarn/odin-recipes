My Favorite Recipe For A Simple Dip

Perfectly sweet and tart on top of a buttery shortbread crust

This lemon bar recipe creates a batch of bars that have a lovely lemony tart filling that rests comfortably atop a generous volume of buttery shortbread. These lemon bars are truly for those who, like myself, are not typically fans of lemon flavored desserts.

Ingredients

  • Roasted Tomatoes:
    • 4 Cups Ripened Cherry Tomatoes
    • 2 Garlic Cloves, Smashed
    • 1⁄4 Cup Extra Virgin Olive Oil
    • 2 Sprigs Fresh Thyme
    • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Whipped Ricotta
    • 15 Ounces Whole Milk
    • 1⁄2 Cup Finely Grated Parmesan
    • Zest from 1 Lemon
    • 1 Teaspoon Dried Oregano
    • 1⁄4 Cup Heavy Cream
    • Salt and Pepper to taste

Instructions:

Step 1.
Preheat the oven to 275 degrees F (135 degrees C)

Step 2.
Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil and place the tomatoes, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper.

Step 3.
Drizzle with olive oil and roast for 2-2.5 hours until the tomatoes soften. Then remove the tomatoes and allow them to cool.

Step 4.
Place the ricotta ingredients in a food processor or blender and pulse until smooth. Taste and season as desired.

Step 5.
Spoon the ricotta spread into a bowl and then spoon the tomatoes and juice in the pan onto the center of the ricotta.

Step 6.
Drizzle the ricotta and tomatoes with a little olive oil, pepper, and salt.

Step 7.
Enjoy the dip with some toasted bread (french bread or sourdough works best for me) and enjoy!
