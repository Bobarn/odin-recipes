My Favorite Recipe For Lemon Bars

Perfectly sweet and tart on top of a buttery shortbread crust

This lemon bar recipe creates a batch of bars that have a lovely lemony tart filling that rests comfortably atop a generous volume of buttery shortbread. These lemon bars are truly for those who, like myself, are not typically fans of lemon flavored desserts.

Ingredients

  • Crust:
    • 2 Cups All-Purpose Flour
    • 1 Cup Butter, Softened
    • 1⁄2 Cup White Sugar (substitute for splenda or monkfruit if needed, I recommend a little less if you substitute)
  • Filling
    • 1 1⁄2 Cups White Wugar
    • 1⁄4 Cup All-Purpose Flour
    • 4 Eggs
    • 2 Lemons Juiced
    • Optional: Zest From Your Two Juiced Lemons. (I personally find that this gives it that extra bit of tartness and helps liven up the flavor a little more)

Instructions:

Step 1.
Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)

Step 2.
To make the crust: Blend 2 cups flour, softened butter, and 1/2 cup sugar in a medium bowl until well combined; press into the bottom of an ungreased 9x13-inch pan.

Step 3.
Bake in the preheated oven until firm and golden, about 15 minutes. Meanwhile, make the filling: Whisk remaining 1 1/2 cups sugar and 1/4 cup flour in a medium bowl. Whisk in eggs, then lemon juice (and the optional zest) until smooth; pour filling over the baked crust.

Step 4.
Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Set the pan aside to cool completely; the bars will firm up as they cool. When cooled, cut into uniform squares.
